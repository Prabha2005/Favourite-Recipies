Rasgulla Recipe

A delicious Bengali dessert made from fresh chhena, soaked in sugar syrup.

Steps to Make Rasgulla

1. Prepare Chhena (Cottage Cheese):

Boil milk and curdle it with lemon juice or vinegar. Drain the curdled milk in a muslin cloth to separate the chhena from the whey. Rinse it with cold water and hang it for 30 minutes to drain excess water.

2. Knead the Chhena:

Knead the chhena until it becomes smooth and soft. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Ensure there are no lumps in the mixture.

3. Shape the Rasgullas:

Divide the kneaded chhena into small equal portions. Roll each portion into a smooth ball without any cracks. If you notice cracks, re-knead it and reshape.

4. Prepare Sugar Syrup:

In a large pan, add water and sugar in a 1:2 ratio. Bring it to a boil and ensure the sugar dissolves completely. Once the syrup is boiling, reduce the heat to medium.

5. Cook the Rasgullas:

Carefully drop the shaped rasgullas into the boiling syrup. Cover the pan and let them cook for 15-20 minutes. The rasgullas will expand, so ensure there's enough space between them. Stir occasionally but gently to prevent them from sticking.

6. Cool and Serve:

Once the rasgullas are cooked, remove them from the syrup and let them cool down. Serve them chilled or at room temperature, soaking them in the syrup.
