Poori Recipe

A popular Indian breakfast dish, poori is a deep-fried bread, soft and fluffy.

Steps to Make Poori

1. Prepare the Dough:

In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, a pinch of salt, and a little oil. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough. Let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

2. Roll the Dough:

Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a small, flat circle. Ensure the dough is evenly rolled to avoid uneven puffing while frying.

3. Heat the Oil:

Heat enough oil in a deep frying pan for the pooris to puff up when fried. The oil should be hot enough but not smoking.

4. Fry the Pooris:

Carefully slide each rolled poori into the hot oil. Gently press it with a slotted spoon so it puffs up. Fry until both sides are golden brown.

5. Serve:

Once fried, remove the pooris from the oil and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve hot with curry, chutney, or potato masala.
